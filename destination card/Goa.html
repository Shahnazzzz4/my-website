About Goa

Goa is a state in western India with coastlines stretching along the Arabian Sea. Its long history as a Portuguese colony prior to 1961 is evident in its preserved 17th-century churches and the areaâ€™s tropical spice plantations. Goa is also known for its beaches, ranging from popular stretches at Baga and Palolem to those in laid-back fishing villages such as Agonda.

Goa had many old names, including Gomantak, Gomant, Govapuri, Gopakapattana, and Govem, as it was known by different names in ancient and medieval times. Historically, it was also known as Sindabur by Arabian geographers and was referred to as "Velha Goa" (Old Goa) by the Portuguese. Some famous things to buy in Goa include cashew nuts, Feni (a local spirit), and Goan spices. You can also find unique handicrafts like Azulejos tiles and coconut crafts, traditional textiles like Kunbi sarees, and local food products such as Bebinca cake and kokum sherbet. Art, music, and local wines are also popular souvenirs

Goa is famous for

Goa is famous for its beaches, vibrant nightlife, unique cuisine, and Portuguese-influenced architecture. Visitors also enjoy water sports, historical churches, and a lively market scene

Featured Destinations

Taj Mahal

UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World. A symbol of eternal love built entirely of white marble.

Agra Fort

Red sandstone fort showcasing Mughal architecture. Contains palaces, mosques, and halls built by different Mughal emperors.

Fatehpur Sikri

Abandoned Mughal city featuring stunning red sandstone architecture. Home to the Buland Darwaza and Jama Masjid.

Itimad-ud-Daulah

Known as the "Baby Taj", this beautiful tomb is often considered a draft of the Taj Mahal with intricate marble inlay work.

Mughlai Cuisine

Indulge in authentic Mughlai dishes like petha (sweet), kebabs, biryani, and traditional street food.

Marble Inlay Work

Shop for exquisite marble handicrafts, leather goods, and carpets. Agra is famous for its marble inlay work (Pietra Dura).
