Agra

The City of Eternal Love and Architectural Marvels

About Agra

Agra, a city on the banks of the Yamuna River in Uttar Pradesh, is home to the magnificent Taj Mahal, one of the Seven Wonders of the World. This historic city was the capital of the Mughal Empire and showcases some of the finest examples of Mughal architecture. From the eternal symbol of love that is the Taj Mahal to the imposing Agra Fort, the city is a treasure trove of history, culture, and architectural brilliance.

The city's rich heritage, combined with its vibrant culture and delicious Mughlai cuisine, makes Agra a must-visit destination for travelers from around the world. Every monument tells a story of the glorious Mughal era, and every street echoes with tales of emperors, artisans, and architectural genius.

History

Agra's history dates back to the 11th century, but it rose to prominence in the 16th century when it became the capital of the Mughal Empire under Emperor Akbar. The city flourished under Mughal rule, becoming a center of art, culture, and architecture.

The most iconic monument, the Taj Mahal, was built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. Completed in 1653, it took 22 years and over 20,000 workers to build this masterpiece of white marble. The Taj Mahal is not just a mausoleum but a symbol of eternal love and architectural perfection.

Agra Fort, another UNESCO World Heritage Site, was initially built by Akbar in 1565 and later expanded by his grandson Shah Jahan. The fort served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors until 1638 when the capital was shifted to Delhi.

Featured Destinations

Taj Mahal

UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World. A symbol of eternal love built entirely of white marble.

Agra Fort

Red sandstone fort showcasing Mughal architecture. Contains palaces, mosques, and halls built by different Mughal emperors.

Fatehpur Sikri

Abandoned Mughal city featuring stunning red sandstone architecture. Home to the Buland Darwaza and Jama Masjid.

Itimad-ud-Daulah

Known as the "Baby Taj", this beautiful tomb is often considered a draft of the Taj Mahal with intricate marble inlay work.

Mughlai Cuisine

Indulge in authentic Mughlai dishes like petha (sweet), kebabs, biryani, and traditional street food.

Marble Inlay Work

Shop for exquisite marble handicrafts, leather goods, and carpets. Agra is famous for its marble inlay work (Pietra Dura).
